## Executive Summary

Acme Corp delivered solid growth in Q3 2025, with total revenue reaching $5.95 million, a 10.2% increase over the same quarter last year. Operating profit grew 8.8% to $3.34 million. Customer retention remained strong, with net revenue retention of 112%, and the company ended the quarter with 4,821 active customers, up 17.5% year-on-year.

Revenue growth was led by Product Line A, which grew 10.5% year-on-year to $4.20 million, while Product Line B contributed $1.75 million, representing 9.4% growth. Regionally, APAC was the fastest-growing market at 14.0% growth, while EMEA grew 6.9%. Total operating expenses grew at 12.0%, outpacing revenue growth and driven primarily by increased investment in Sales & Marketing and Engineering. Operating margin contracted modestly from 56.9% to 56.1%, reflecting the higher expense growth rate.

Acme Corp ended the quarter with 4,821 active customers, representing 17.5% growth year-on-year. New customer acquisition totaled 387 in the quarter, up from 310 in the prior year period. Net revenue retention of 112% indicates that existing customers continue to expand their usage and represents a 4 percentage point improvement over the prior year. The quarterly churn rate of 1.95% remains within historical norms, though absolute churn volume increased to 94 customers from 88 in the prior year.

Acme Corp added 24 net new employees during the quarter, ending at 328 total headcount, representing 9.7% growth year-on-year. This expansion is broadly in line with revenue growth. New hires increased 33.3% compared to the prior year period, while departures also rose 33.3% to 8 employees, both movements warranting continued monitoring to ensure retention and hiring quality remain aligned with company strategy.

Sales & Marketing expense growth of 14.3% outpaced overall revenue growth of 10.2% this quarter, and leadership should assess whether current sales capacity investments are positioned to deliver expected returns. Additionally, the elevated levels of both new hires and departures relative to the prior year merit review to ensure organizational stability and hiring effectiveness.
